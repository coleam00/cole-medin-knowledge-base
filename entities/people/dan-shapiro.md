# Dan Shapiro

Dan Shapiro is the writer Cole credits with two connected framings that anchor how this catalog talks about autonomy in coding. First, he is the person who carried the manufacturing metaphor over to software: "Dan Shapiro was the one to apply this to code bases and generative AI" ([0:02:19]), giving Cole the vocabulary for the [Dark Factory](../../concepts/dark-factory.md) experiment. Second, and more influential day to day, he authored the analogy Cole reaches for whenever the question is how much to trust an agent: "I really love Dan's blog post here because he gives a really good analogy comparing the different levels of self-driving cars to using generative AI for coding" ([0:02:54]).

That self-driving-levels model is why Dan sits behind Cole's central caution that the most autonomous setup is not the best one. Cole introduces the whole argument through him: "the best explanation I found for the different ways we can use coding agents is from Dan Shapiro. So in this blog post" ([0:00:00]). The framing maps directly onto [Human in the Loop](../../concepts/human-in-the-loop.md), the choice between [Deterministic Workflows](../../concepts/deterministic-workflows.md) and full autonomy, and the design of an [AI Coding Harness](../../concepts/ai-coding-harness.md) that dials autonomy up or down per task. It is a load-bearing reference for [Agentic Workflow Engineering](../../concepts/agentic-workflow-engineering.md) and for reading [The Evolution of AI Coding](../../concepts/the-evolution-of-ai-coding.md) as graduated levels rather than a single leap.
