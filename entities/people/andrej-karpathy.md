# Andrej Karpathy

Andrej Karpathy is the AI engineer whose offhand naming has repeatedly seeded whole practices in Cole's world. Most famously, "Andre Karpathy coined the term vibe coding" ([0:00:00]), the February-2025 tweet that defined a moment in [The Evolution of AI Coding](../../concepts/the-evolution-of-ai-coding.md). His second durable contribution is the pattern of using an LLM to build and maintain personal knowledge bases: Cole quotes him directly, "Karpathy starts by saying something I'm finding very useful recently is using LLM to build personal knowledge bases for various topics of research interest" ([0:00:29]). That idea, shared as a single gist, became a phenomenon: "This single markdown document in GitHub called it gist got to 40,000 stars" ([0:00:19]).

Karpathy functions in this catalog as the origin point Cole builds on and, gently, argues with. He is credited as the source of [Vibe Coding](../../concepts/vibe-coding.md) and of [The LLM Wiki](../../concepts/the-llm-wiki.md), and Cole treats his releases as channel-defining events: "when Karpathy releases LLM Wiki architecture, I covered that" ([0:02:10]). But Cole's engagement is not pure agreement. His own [Second Brain](../../concepts/second-brain.md) exists precisely because he thinks the raw Karpathy wiki "doesn't scale," and he notes with interest that Karpathy "is spending more of his tokens with his agents manipulating knowledge like Markdown and Obsidian instead of manipulating code" ([0:02:22]), a shift that anticipates [Context Engineering](../../concepts/context-engineering.md) and [Progressive Disclosure](../../concepts/progressive-disclosure.md) over knowledge. Notably, Karpathy later endorsed the successor term too: "Context engineering was even condoned by Andre who originally coined Vibe coding" ([0:02:21]).
