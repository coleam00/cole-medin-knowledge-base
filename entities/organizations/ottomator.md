# oTTomator

oTTomator (ottomator.ai) is the earlier community Cole built before [Dynamous](./dynamous.md), positioned as a hub for people getting serious about AI agents. He frames it broadly: "we are a One-Stop shop for you to get all of the tools networking knowledge and resources to succeed in this super fast evolving industry of AI" ([0:03:48]). Where Dynamous later leaned into a structured course and the Context Engineering Hub, oTTomator's center of gravity was open, community-driven building, embodying the [Community-Driven Development](../../concepts/community-driven-development.md) pattern that recurs across Cole's work.

Its flagship was the **Live Agent Studio**, a public gallery where community members shipped and shared working agents, exercising [Chat Interfaces](../../concepts/chat-interfaces.md), [Streaming Responses](../../concepts/streaming-responses.md), and [Agentic RAG](../../concepts/agentic-rag.md) against a common, [provider-independent](../../concepts/provider-independence.md) surface. Cole used it to drive participation directly, hosting a large competition: "my platform automator is hosting a hackathon competition with $5,000 in cash prizes" ([0:00:00]). The studio doubled as a living answer to [What Is an AI Agent](../../concepts/what-is-an-ai-agent.md), a catalog of real, runnable examples rather than definitions.

In the arc of Cole's channel, oTTomator is the predecessor that seeded the community model he scaled up in [Dynamous](./dynamous.md): a place to gather early adopters, run events, and turn scattered experimentation into shared, reusable agent building.
