# AI YouTube Is Only Claude Hype Now

Cole opens with the observation that the only AI content performing well on YouTube right now is Claude-focused news: every new model, feature, or viral plugin triggers a rush of near-identical videos from dozens of creators. He respects those creators but doesn't want to be one of them, his channel has always been deeper, teaching the principles of building a second brain, AI agents, and coding assistants in ways that actually scale, even though the algorithm favors news chasing.

To lean into that, he's launching a live-streaming cadence: Mondays, Thursdays, and Saturdays at 9am Central, each stream 1.5-2.5 hours, as an experiment for a few weeks. He'll keep his usual weekly (Wednesday) videos too, and still cover genuinely important releases, but only after tinkering with them and incorporating them into his own systems, not within four hours of launch. A key reason he devalues pure news videos: you can now just ask your coding agent how a new feature works. He points to Claude Code's built-in skill for searching its own up-to-date documentation, which can summarize new features (slash commands, the memory/'dreaming' system, routines) and help you fold them into your own workflows.

The streams will center on real systems: building Archon (his open-source harness builder for AI coding) live with the audience, and the Dark Factory experiment, a codebase he hands entirely to AI coding assistants where no human writes or reviews code. He's also considering cutting the streams into more polished regular videos afterward. The through-line: deliver maximum durable, high-leverage value amid the sea of Claude-hype content.
